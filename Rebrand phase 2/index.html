Brand Evolution — Phase 2

A Brand Growing
With Our Community

Positive Partnerships has always stood for connection, inclusion, and belonging. This evolution of our visual identity brings those values to life — drawing from the rich, diverse colour world of our Diversity Wheel to create a brand that is warm, dynamic, and unmistakably ours.

The Mark

The Evolved Logo

The PP mark has been reimagined with the geometric, faceted quality of the Diversity Wheel — blues, purples, and teals merge in a low-poly mosaic that gives the familiar form new depth and energy. The wordmark and tagline remain anchored in our trusted brand typeface.

The Mark

The PP letterforms — a trusted symbol for our community — now rendered in a multi-colour faceted style drawn directly from the structure and palette of the Diversity Wheel.

The Wordmark

"Positive Partnerships" in deep brand purple. Authoritative, warm, and instantly recognisable to our existing audience across schools and families.

The Tagline

"working together to support autistic school-aged students" — set in teal, grounding every application of the logo in our mission and the communities we serve.

The Story

Evolution, Not Revolution

Our existing brand has served us well — it's recognisable, trusted, and connected to years of meaningful work with families and schools across Australia. This rebrand builds on that foundation rather than replacing it.

🔵

Familiar Foundation

The PP mark and the Positive Partnerships wordmark are retained — our community knows and trusts these. The evolution is in the detail: new depth, new colour, new energy drawn from our most important educational resource.

🌈

Rooted in the Diversity Wheel

The Diversity Wheel is central to our educational model. By drawing its colour palette and geometric shapes into our brand, we make our values visible in everything we produce — connecting design to purpose.

✦

Dynamic & Inclusive

A spectrum of colour signals that autism is not one experience — it is rich, varied, and uniquely human. Our brand now reflects that diversity with confidence, warmth, and clarity.

The Design Foundation

The Diversity Wheel

The Diversity Wheel represents the six key areas that shape everyday experiences for autistic people. Adapted from the original wheel by autistic cartoonist Rebecca Burgess, it is both a powerful educational tool and a visual expression of the richness and variety of human experience.

The wheel's full-spectrum colour palette and its geometric, faceted structure are the direct source of our brand's new design language — from the low-poly triangle patterns in our presentation backgrounds, to the multi-coloured gradient in the refreshed PP mark, to the vibrant accent corners in our collateral.

By placing the Diversity Wheel at the centre of our visual identity, we ensure our brand is always connected to the people and experiences it exists to serve.

Visual Language

Design Elements

Three core design elements work together across all Positive Partnerships materials — creating a flexible visual system that is instantly recognisable while adapting to every context.

Geometric Low-Poly Patterns

Triangular facets drawn from the Diversity Wheel structure create dynamic, energetic backgrounds. They suggest depth and complexity without cluttering communication — ideal for slide backgrounds, cover pages, and section dividers.

Petal & Oval Photo Frames

Photography is cropped into soft petal and oval shapes — connecting directly to the PP mark and our existing petal design motif. This creates warmth and human connection while reinforcing the brand's core form language.

Diversity Wheel Corner Accents

Warm-toned arc fragments from the Diversity Wheel appear as corner and edge accents on collateral and resources. They signal our connection to the wheel, add energy to layouts, and introduce the full spectrum into every communication.

Where To Next

The Path Forward

This phase has established the visual direction for the evolved brand. The next steps bring it to life across all our channels and materials.

🎨

Finalise the Mark

Lock in the final version of the PP mark and wordmark, confirm the geometric colour treatment, and produce master files in all required formats (SVG, PNG, EPS) for deployment across teams.

📖

Brand Guidelines

Document the full visual system — logo usage rules, colour palette with CMYK/RGB/HEX values, typography hierarchy, photo treatment guidelines, and pattern usage — in a formal Brand Guidelines document.

🌐

Website & Digital

Apply the evolved brand to positivepartnerships.com.au — refreshing the header, colour system, typography, and imagery treatment to reflect the new direction while preserving existing content and SEO.
