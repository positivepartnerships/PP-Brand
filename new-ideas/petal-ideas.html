Exploring the Petal Motif

Ideas for evolving the Diversity Wheel's central bloom into a flexible brand shape system — for slides, web, print, and beyond. Each concept here is derived from the overlapping petal forms at the heart of the wheel.

The Source: Diversity Wheel

The central bloom is made of overlapping, translucent petal shapes radiating from a single point. Each petal is a different hue — together they form a complete spectrum. This is the visual metaphor we're extracting from.

What it represents: Every petal is unique in colour and position, but they overlap and connect to create something whole. That's neurodiversity. That's partnership.

Idea 06

Logo Concepts

Four directions for evolving the PP logo with the petal motif. Each keeps what works — the "pp" letterforms and the purple identity — while bringing in the diversity bloom story.

Current Logo
Purple circle mark with connected pp descenders + wordmark + tagline

A. Bloom Halo

The existing logo mark stays intact — the bloom radiates behind it as a halo of petals. Minimal disruption to brand recognition while introducing the diversity motif. The petals peek out around the edges, suggesting depth and spectrum. Works well at large sizes; at small sizes the halo fades gracefully.

Evolutionary Safe Dual-use

B. Bloom Mark

The purple circle is replaced entirely by the petal bloom. The "pp" sits directly over the overlapping petals in dark purple. The bloom becomes the logo mark — every colour visible, the spectrum is the brand. Bolder move, much more distinctive. The overlapping translucency is the hero.

Bold Distinctive Modern

C. Partnership Petals

Two clusters of petals — one cool (purple/blue), one warm (orange/red/yellow) — lean toward each other and overlap in the centre. The overlap zone is green: something new created by coming together. A shared descender anchors them like the original pp. Abstract but deeply meaningful. The two halves of a partnership, meeting in understanding.

Conceptual Meaningful Abstract

D. Spectrum Wordmark

A stacked wordmark where "positive" carries the full spectrum gradient and "partnerships" holds strong in white. A small bloom sits to the left as the icon mark. The spectrum bar replaces the traditional underline. Designed for dark backgrounds — bold, contemporary, and the spectrum tells the neurodiversity story through the type itself. Works beautifully for digital, hero sections, and event signage.

Digital-first Contemporary Signage

Idea 07

Animated Brand Elements

Subtle motion adds life to the brand. These are CSS-only animations — lightweight, scalable, and ready to drop into websites, presentations, or digital signage.

Floating Petals

Translucent petals drift gently upward at different speeds and angles — like seeds on a breeze. Each petal is a random spectrum colour with slight rotation. Use as an ambient website background, loading screen, or event signage atmosphere.

Atmospheric Website Signage

Spectrum Fade

A single petal smoothly cycles through every colour in the diversity spectrum — the full journey takes 12 seconds. Represents the breadth of neurodiversity in one continuous flow. Use as a hero accent, loading indicator, or favicon animation.

Hero Accent Loading Favicon

Flutter in the Breeze

Petals gently sway and compress as if caught in a light breeze — each on its own rhythm so the motion feels organic, not mechanical. Use as a decorative banner element or section divider that's alive with subtle movement.

Decorative Banner Organic

Breathing Bloom

The full bloom gently pulses — each petal slightly extends and contracts in a staggered wave, like a living thing breathing. Calm, meditative, and deeply on-brand. Perfect for a hero background, welcome screen, or calming visual in workshop waiting rooms.

Hero Calming Welcome Screen

Ripple Pulse

Coloured rings ripple outward from a central bloom — like the impact of a partnership radiating through a community. Each ring is a different spectrum colour. Use for notification moments, page transitions, or to draw the eye to a call-to-action.

Notification CTA Impact

Slow Spin

The bloom rotates almost imperceptibly — one full revolution every 30 seconds. You barely notice it's moving, but it gives the logo a living, organic quality. Ideal for a website watermark, loading state, or ambient desktop wallpaper element.

Watermark Loading Ambient
