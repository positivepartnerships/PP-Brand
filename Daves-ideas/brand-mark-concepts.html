Brand Mark Concepts — Negative Space

Four directions exploring how negative space can embed meaning into the Positive Partnerships mark. Each concept hides a petal — or something more — in the space between shapes.

The best logos say two things at once
Concept 1

The Lens — two circles, one vision

Two overlapping circles — one for home, one for school. Where they meet, the negative space reveals a vertical petal: the place where partnership creates something neither could alone. Minimal, geometric, universally legible.

Design Notes The two circles reference the Venn diagram — a universal symbol of overlap and shared purpose. The vesica piscis (the lens shape) has centuries of use as a symbol of union and new life. At small sizes the petal disappears and two clean circles remain — maintaining legibility as a favicon or app icon. The mark works monochromatically and at any scale.
Concept 2

The PP Petal — hidden in the initials

The twin bowls of the initials P+P face each other like two hands cupped together. Where they overlap, the letterforms disappear and the petal reveals itself in the negative space. A secret hidden in the name — you can't unsee it once you spot it.

Design Notes The initials PP read naturally into the bowls of the letterforms. Two white crescents flank a central petal — read as: two partners (crescents) creating something together (petal). The straight inner edges of the Ps create satisfying geometric tension with the curved outer arcs. Strong at all sizes; at favicon size it reads as a clean circle-ish badge.
Concept 3

The Cradle — held within

Two sweeping arcs — two arms, or two paths — curve around each other. In the space they protect between them, a petal grows. Warmth and support made visible: the child held by the community.

Design Notes The two arcs meet at the same top and bottom points — visually tight, a closed system of support. The negative space petal is pointed at both ends (classic petal form) and perfectly centred. The rounded stroke caps make the mark feel warm rather than rigid. Without the background, the mark works as two flowing paths — use on dark backgrounds for maximum impact.
Concept 4

Petal Within Petal — growth within growth

A petal frame — its outer edge solid white, its interior hollow — reveals a second, smaller petal in the negative space within. The outer petal is the support network. The inner petal is the child. The white frame is the act of partnership itself.

Design Notes Most directly connected to the existing petal brand language — can evolve the current mark rather than replace it. The frame/ring approach is extremely versatile: vary the frame thickness to shift from delicate to bold. The inner petal can also hold a small icon or dot (representing the child or a specific program). Works well as a circular badge, envelope seal, or document watermark.
