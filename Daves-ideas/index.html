Exploring the Petal Motif

How the diversity wheel's petal forms can extend into brand marks, patterns, interface elements, and neurodiversity storytelling — celebrating every mind.

Brand Sandpit · Dave's Ideas · March 2026
Concept 1

The Petal Mark — Simplified Brand Marks

Reducing the diversity wheel to its essential petal form creates versatile logomarks that work across digital and print applications at any size.

Design Notes

Each mark uses the same petal path M 50,50 C 38,40 28,22 50,8 C 72,22 62,40 50,50 Z rotated around a shared centre. The key decisions are petal count and colour palette:

  • 6-petal purple — tight, authoritative; ideal for small contexts (favicon, document watermark).
  • 12-petal rainbow — expressive, celebratory; best for large-format, social covers.
  • Reversed (white) — for dark backgrounds, merchandise, embossed print.
  • Accent triad — Lime, Orange, Red only; energetic, youth-facing tone.
Concept 2

Corner & Edge Treatments

A quarter-wheel fan of petals in a corner, or a vertical edge strip, instantly brands a slide or document without dominating the content.

Design Notes

Quarter-wheel and edge treatments solve a common problem: how to brand templates without fighting content. The petal fan is clipped to its corner, giving an organic softness that contrasts well with the rectangular slide format. Use the rainbow version for participant-facing materials; use the purple-monochrome version for internal or executive documents.

Concept 3

Petal Scatter Pattern

Petals used as a repeating texture — perfect as a background on slides, web sections, or print collateral. Semi-transparency keeps them supportive, not dominant.

Design Notes

  • Light version — mist lavender base with purple-tinted petals. Ideal for speaker notes pages, handout backgrounds.
  • Dark version — deep purple base with muted rainbow petals. Striking for title slides, hero web sections.
  • Animated — gentle drift for web use. Subtle enough to not distract; adds life to a page on load.
Concept 4

Neural Network & Petal Fusion

Petal shapes as synaptic nodes, connected by flowing bezier curves — celebrating the beauty of neurodivergent minds through the language of brain imaging and botanical form.

Design Notes

The petal's organic curve echoes the dendritic branching of neurons. Bezier paths flow between petal tips, and animated stroke-dashoffset creates the illusion of electrical signals travelling along pathways. Colours reference fMRI brain imaging palettes: deep navy backgrounds, electric blues and teals for active pathways, warm amber for high-activity nodes.

  • Use cases: conference keynote opening slide, website hero, printed poster.
  • Accessible alternative: provide a static version without animation for motion-sensitive users.
Concept 5

The Infinity Spectrum

The neurodiversity infinity symbol — universally recognised — filled with the diversity wheel's colours, with petal accents scattered along its path.

Design Notes

The infinity/figure-8 is the established symbol of the neurodiversity movement. Pairing it with the petal motif creates a bridge between PP's brand language and a globally understood icon. The rainbow gradient version is emotionally expressive; the single-colour version is practical for single-colour print (letterheads, stamps, embossing).

  • Tagline "Every Mind Matters" is set in Raleway 300 — lightweight against the bold mark.
  • Small petal accents scattered along the path echo the full diversity wheel without copying it directly.
Concept 6

Petal as UI Element

Petals as functional interface components — icon containers, section dividers, progress indicators, and watermarks — showing how the motif can be systematised into a design system.

Design Notes

  • Petal icon containers — clip-path on a div creates the petal shape for icon backgrounds. More distinctive than circles or squares.
  • Progress petals — each step fills a petal. Works for multi-step forms, module completion trackers.
  • Watermark header — a large, very-low-opacity petal behind a section heading. Subtle texture.
  • Section divider — a row of small petals replaces the standard horizontal rule.

Application Mockups

Application Mockups

How these concepts look in real-world contexts — PowerPoint title slide, web hero section, and document cover.

Design Notes

These mockups illustrate the three primary brand touchpoints: presentation slides (the most common output), web/digital, and formal documents. The petal decoration is consistently placed at the right or bottom-right — establishing a spatial convention that makes templated materials instantly recognisable as PP.
